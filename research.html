Market Reactions to Mandatory Climate Disclosure: Evidence from Changes in the EU Taxonomy

with Marie-Theres von Schickfus

Mandatory disclosure of environmental information has become a popular regulatory tool, aiming to reduce information asymmetries between firms and financial markets. This paper examines market reactions to mandatory climate disclosure due to the EU Taxonomy regulation, which requires companies to report according to a standardized classification system. Based on sector classification codes, activities are labeled as “Taxonomy-eligible” and can attain the label of “Taxonomy-aligned” if they fulfill performance criteria. Applying an event study methodology, we exploit the variation in sectors defined as eligible following amendments to the initially communicated eligible sectors. We find a significant negative stock market reaction to the amendment in 2023, which added subsectors in aviation and automobile manufacturing. The results are consistent with the equity market perceiving net costs for firms which are required to disclose under the EU Taxonomy, due to implementation costs or reputational risks. These findings are relevant in the discussion of the impacts of sustainable finance policies.
